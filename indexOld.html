We need a title. Mission Statement

Powe & White Family Lawyers is leading Hunter Valley firm which specialises in the area of family and de-facto law. Our firm represents a unique mix of experience and new thinking, combining years of legal knowledge with innovative ideas to resolve complex legal issues. Powe & White Family Lawyers believe in being part of the solution. We understand the importance of providing our clients not only with expert advice, but also guidance during what is often a difficult time in life. Centrally located in the heart of the Hunter Valley, Powe & White Family Lawyers services clients throughout the Maitland, Newcastle, Port Stephens and greater New England areas. With the use of technology, Powe & White Lawyers services client’s outside of these areas, to include clients who reside in Sydney, on the mid North Coast, in other states in Australia and internationally. Powe & White Family Lawyers is committed to helping resolve our clients’ matters promptly, cost-effectively and where possible without the need of going to Court. To organise an appointment with Powe & White Family Lawyers, please contact us.

At Powe & White Family Lawyers, we understand that the thought of legal fees coupled with a legal problem can be challenging. Our lawyers undertake a process to ensure that all clients are fully made aware of our fees charged for the worked performed on their behalf. Our lawyers do this not only as we have a statutory responsibility to ensure that fees are disclosed, but also because legal fees are usually an important consideration in the issues in dispute. Legal fees are usually paid from; • The client; • The client’s family members or friends; • Other third parties such as the Legal Aid Commission of New South Wales (see Legal Aid below), litigation lenders and other financial institutions; • Proceeds of any property settlement At Powe & White Family Lawyers, we understand that not all clients have the money readily available to them to pay our fees up front, but may require time to repay our fees or that our fees be deducted on any form of property settlement. Our lawyers are always happy to consider these options with clients. We recommend that you contact us and make an appointment to speak with one of our lawyers to discuss your matter, which includes a discussion relating to our fees.

Parenting
Arrangements

In most cases, parents and other family members understand the need to put a child’s safety and emotional well-being first. Parental responsibility and living arrangements for children in separated families (formerly referred to as “child custody”, “residence” , “contact” or even “access”) need to be considered and decided upon following a relationship breakdown. By law the best interests of the child are always the most important consideration

Pre-Nuptial
Agreements

By entering into a Binding Financial Agreement (BFA), more commonly known as a Prenuptial Agreement or ‘pre-nups’, you can determine before you marry how your assets will be allocated should your marriage breakdown. Some people describe this process as if they were planning for a will.
more
 

Child
Support

The majority of children in Australia, of separated families, are covered by the Child Support scheme. Most commonly the payment of child support for children in separated families is either agreed upon by each parent, or otherwise assessed and monitored by the Child Support Agency rather than the Courts.
