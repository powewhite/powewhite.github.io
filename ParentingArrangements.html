Parenting Arrangements

Parental responsibility and living arrangements for a child or children in separated families (formerly referred to as “child custody”, “residence” , “contact” or even “access”) need to be considered and decided upon following a relationship breakdown.

By law the best interests of the child are always the most important consideration.

At Powe & White Family Lawyers, our lawyers will ask you to consider the following points when considering the post separation parenting arrangements for children;

  • The safety, welfare and protection of children
  • Where the children will live
  • How often the children spend time with each of their parents
  • How practicable the arrangements are for the children
  • The sharing of special event days such as birthdays, Christmas, and school holidays
  • The location of where the children changeover to the other parent
  • The frequency and times for communication between the children and the parents
  • The school or day care centre that the children will attend
  • Any other special requirement for the children such as their surname or medical treatment they receive

The above matters are of great importance when considering the long term parenting arrangements for the children which should be considered prior to any final agreement being reached.

Some families also include details of the role of grandparents, step parents, and other important people in the lives of children when considering the long term parenting arrangements for the children.
