Pre-Nuptial Agreements

Sometimes in life it is best to plan for every situation.

By entering into a Binding Financial Agreement (BFA), more commonly known as a Prenuptial Agreement or ‘pre-nups’, you can determine before you marry how your assets will be allocated should your marriage breakdown. Some people describe this process as if they were planning for a will.

The benefits of having a Prenuptial Agreement is to give each party to the relationship certainty and the reassurance of what the other will receive upon any separation. This process usually prevents parties disputing property after a separation, saving time, money and more heart ache.

Binding Financial Agreements can also be made during a relationship. The Binding Financial Agreement acts in a similar way to a Prenuptial Agreement, in as much as it sets out in black and white what each party will receive by way of settlement upon any future separation.

Prenuptial Agreements and Binding Financial Agreements should not simply be considered for movie stars and celebrities.

For those people who have been in unsuccessful relationships before or who perhaps have significant wealth prior to entering into a marriage or who simply desire the certainty of knowing they will not end up in Court fighting over the division of assets if a relationship does not work out, Prenuptial Agreements and Binding Financial Agreements can be a very useful and welcomed legal document.

The cost outlaid in planning well for your future might be a cost saved into the future.
