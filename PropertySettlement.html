Property Settlement

A separation your former partner can be a difficult and emotional process. The added layer of unravelling your finances can further complicate your separation. Ensuring that you receive expert advice as to your entitlements to resolve your joint financial affairs is one of the most important steps after separation to enable you to move on to the next chapter of your life.

Property and financial settlements can be complex and require specialist skill and experience to secure your financial future in view of changing family circumstances.

The skill of our family lawyers at dealing with complex property settlements enhances their knowledge in less complex property settlements, such as those involving;

  • • Residential property

  • • Motor Vehicles

  • • Boats

  • • Shares

  • • Bank accounts

  • • Standard superannuation funds

  • • Furniture

  • • Personal debt and credit card debts

Our family lawyers are highly regarded and experienced in analysing property settlement issues including complex financial arrangements involving;

  • • Trusts

  • • Companies

  • • Other corporate structures

  • • Commercial and investment properties

  • • Self managed superannuation funds

Our lawyers are able to clearly inform you the range of your entitlements at law and the possible outcome of a property settlement case if it was necessary for a Court to become involved.

Powe & White Family Lawyers specialise in representation in cases which go to Court where the division of assets cannot be resolved privately.

Property settlement and financial matters often extend beyond just the final division of assets.

Other aspects of property settlements include;

  • • Advice on a Will, Power of Attorney and other Estate Planning

  • • Advice in relation to a transfer of real estate or superannuation

  • • Taking action to secure and preserve assets through injunctions prior to final property settlement, particularly where there is some risk of someone disposing of your asset or decreasing its value prior to any final settlement

Above all, we believe it is extremely important to offer you realistic advice, with the intention of finalising matters to your satisfaction as early as possible and ensuring a clean financial break from your partner in circumstances of a separation.
