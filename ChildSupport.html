Child Support

One of the most commonly asked questions after a separation is how to financially support a child from your relationship. Ensuring that a fair and equitable arrangement is made following a separation is important for both parents and more importantly your children.

For most parents, contacting the Child Support Agency is the first step after a separation to receive advice about the receipt and payment of child support.

Child support is a complex and not easily understood system of requiring one parent to make payment to the other parent in financial support for any child of a relationship.

The purpose of the Child Support Agency was initially designed to simplify the process of parents paying and receiving child support, however many parents report that the child support system is complicated and often leaves you feeling disillusioned with the contributions received and made by the other parent.

At Powe & White Family Lawyers, we have extensive experience in working with the now quite complex child support laws. We understand that the payment of money by one partner to the other is often a sensitive issue so we will assist you to understand the law and its effect on your situation.

At Powe & White Family Lawyers, we can also assist you in the preparation of Child Support Agreements. A Child Support Agreement is a different approach to the payment and receipt of child support which allows parents to “opt-out” of the complex Child Support Agency System.

Child Support Agreements are becoming increasingly more sought after as a means to enable parents to fairly agree upon how child support is paid, how it is received and the amount of direct and indirect child support is paid.
