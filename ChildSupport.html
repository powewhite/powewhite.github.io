Child Support

The majority of children of separated families in Australia are covered by the Child Support scheme. Most commonly, the payment of child support for children in separated families is either agreed upon by each parent or otherwise assessed and monitored by the Child Support Agency rather than the Courts.

The amount of child support payable is different in each case, is dependant upon the specific circumstances of the family and its income, and is based on the application of a statutory formula.

The introduction of the Child Support scheme in 1989 was designed to simplify the process, many variations to the standard formula have been developed over the years since then to accommodate differing family circumstances.

Binding Child Support Agreements are becoming an increasingly more common solution to the payment of child support.

Binding Child Support Agreements allow each party input as to how much and how frequently child support is paid. This flexibility allows parties to recognise their financial circumstances to better enable a just and equitable account of child support.
